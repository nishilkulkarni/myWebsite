An image is worth 16x16 words: Vision Transformers for image recognition at scale
Nishil Kulkarni

A Python3 (PyTorch) implementation of The Vision Transformer (ViT). The model applies the transformer architecture, originally designed for natural language processing, to image classification tasks. Instead of using convolutional layers like CNNs, ViT splits an image into patches, embeds them as tokenized inputs, and processes them through self-attention mechanisms to capture long-range dependencies.

Thoracic Disease Detection in Chest X-Rays Using CNN
Nishil Kulkarni

Python3 (Pytorch) implementation of CheXNet. The model takes a chest X-ray image as input and outputs the probability of each thoracic disease along with a likelihood map of pathologies. Also developed a slightly-improved model which achieves a mean AUROC of 0.847 (v.s. 0.841 of the original CheXNet).

Connect 4 AI
Nishil Kulkarni

Developed a Connect 4 AI using the minimax algorithm with alpha-beta pruning, integrating heuristic functions for move ordering, state evaluation, and advanced strategies like column weighting and sequence analysis to optimize decision-making.

Statistical Modeling of Agricultural Derivatives Trading and Its Impact on Global Food Security
Nishil Kulkarni, Dr. R.L Manogna
ArXiV

Statistical modelling to analyze impact of derivatives trading in agricultural commodities on food security. We sought to empirically investigate this complicated problem and influence policy choices to ensure a more stable and secure food system by analyzing the role of financialization in global food markets.
